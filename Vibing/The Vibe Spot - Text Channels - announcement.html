Cinnamon Pumpkin Bread Mar-05-21 09:29 PM
Also, I realize that finding scholarships may be a bit challenging especially because so many deadlines have passed. So, here is a quick rundown of possible areas to look for scholarships: -- As seen on any school TV, log into Xello from the student links page using your school email and password (it's through google). Then click on College Planning > Search for Local Scholarships a little ways down the page. There, they have a list of scholarships in chronological order of their due dates. Check for any you are interested in/are eligible for, and try and meet the deadline! -- If you have any relatives that have attended the school in which you are going for, make sure to show that when applying as it increases your chances of gaining an additional scholarship or two -- If your parents/relatives work for any companies, check to see if they have any scholarships. Most will, and the competition (most of the time) won't be too high. -- If you are working, check to see if your work has any scholarships -- College Board offers a variety of scholarships that can even be narrowed down through information on your College Board profile. https://bigfuture.collegeboard.org/scholarship-search# -- Chegg offers a large variety of scholarships along with book rentals https://www.chegg.com/scholarships#
